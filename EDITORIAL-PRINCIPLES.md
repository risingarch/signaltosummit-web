# Signal to Summit — Editorial Principles (Locked March 5, 2026)

These principles apply site-wide to all copy. Locked by Jaiah.

1. **No negative distinctions on the web** — Do not frame Signal to Summit by what competitors lack.
2. **No deficit framing** — Frame what Jeff gets, not what his team is missing. Never "your team never mapped" or "your team consistently underestimates."
3. **No internal language** — No "founder's thesis," "RMN," framework names, or any term Jeff wouldn't use in his own meetings.
4. **Copy density must match its container** — Homepage scroll sections get tight, punchy copy. Long-form pages can breathe. Don't put thesis-density copy in a card.
5. **Buyer questions must hold for both fix-it and build-it scenarios** — Every service question works whether Jeff is trying to fix something broken or build something new.
6. **Credibility line (locked):** "Ten years inside a top-10 retailer, seven building retail media."
